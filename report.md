# DataPeople — Recurring Problems Report
**Source:** 11 meeting transcripts, March 3–24, 2026
**Meetings analyzed:** weekly syncs, product standups, sales 1-on-1s, marketing/product sync, client call, quarterly strategy session

---

## Problem 1: Slow and missed responses to inbound leads

### Summary
Sales reps are drowning in email — 200–300 messages per day per person — and cannot distinguish hot leads from noise fast enough. The result is deals lost directly to competitors who respond faster.

### Occurrences across transcripts

| Date | Meeting | What happened |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Mar 3 | Weekly Sync | LogisPro handoff between Katie and Max unclear; no one knows if Max ever reached out |
| Mar 5 | Sales 1-on-1 | Peterson missed AgroTech Systems' email for 2 days (200 unread); they signed with HRbox. Lost deal: $80K |
| Mar 5 | Sales 1-on-1 | Elena spends 1.5–2 hrs/day sorting email; Nick (new rep) missed follow-ups with 2 clients on his first week |
| Mar 10 | Weekly Sync | Max sent LogisPro's email to the wrong address (copied from an old thread with a typo); client waited 2 weeks |
| Mar 17 | Weekly Sync | CargoTransit (400 employees) lost to BambooHR — Danny's technical questions to Dave sat unanswered for 2 weeks; competitor responded in 2 days |
| Mar 18 | Sales 1-on-1 | FinServe request ($150K/year potential) sat 3 days while Katie was buried; by the time she responded, they were talking to a competitor. Anna flagged 5+ more similar delays |

### Who raised it
- **Anna Sullivan (VP Sales):** primary voice across all 1-on-1s and weekly syncs
- **Paul Crawford (CEO):** acknowledged the problem, repeatedly promised to talk to Dave about a technical fix — no resolution by Mar 24

### Impact
- At least 3 confirmed lost deals (AgroTech, CargoTransit, FinServe) totaling hundreds of thousands in potential ARR
- Katie (described as the best rep) told Anna she's considering leaving
- Q1 ARR target missed ($1.12M vs $1.2M goal); slow response cited as a main cause

### Why it persists
The CRM and email are separate systems. Reps run conversations in email and are supposed to manually log them in the CRM — they don't have time. No prioritization layer exists to surface urgent emails. Proposed fixes (email rules, hiring an assistant) were evaluated and rejected as insufficient or unscalable. Paul committed to discussing a technical solution with Dave but no action was taken within the observed period.

## Problem 2: Marketing landing page blocked by dev capacity for 3+ weeks

### Summary
Marketing has needed a dedicated HR analytics landing page since at least early March. A finished design has existed since January 15. The page was repeatedly blocked by engineering's stated lack of capacity, while competitors launched equivalent pages in the same period.

### Occurrences across transcripts

| Date | Meeting | What happened |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Mar 3 | Weekly Sync | Marina first raises the landing page need. Dave says no capacity. Paul brokers a compromise: Marina preps requirements, Dave tries mid-March. No guarantees |
| Mar 7 | Product Standup | Natalie says Elena from marketing has asked 3 times already. Kevin shuts it down — not in the sprint |
| Mar 10 | Weekly Sync | Marina says she sent the brief to Dave on Wednesday. Dave never saw it (sent by email). Dave asks her to resend via team chat instead |
| Mar 12 | Marketing/Product Sync | Full dedicated meeting on the topic. Reveals the design has been done since January 15 and sat in the task tracker as "To Do" unseen. Decision: hire a freelancer, Kevin coordinates, Dave reviews |
| Mar 17 | Weekly Sync | Dave estimates 3 days without calculator, 1 week with. Nobody available until end of March. Paul says skip calculator, target first week of April |
| Mar 19 | Product Standup | Elena still asking Natalie for dev help; Kevin holds the line — go to Dave if you want to escalate |
| Mar 24 | Weekly Sync | PeopleForce (direct competitor) launched an identical page with calculator, case studies, and video — and is already running Google Ads on "HR analytics." Marina furious. Dave promises developer Jenny starts April 1 |

### Who raised it
- **Marina Wolfe (VP Marketing):** raised it in every single weekly sync and dedicated meeting
- **Kevin Larson (Head of Product):** consistent gatekeeper, pushes back on unplanned requests
- **Dave Kowalski (CTO):** blocked it repeatedly citing capacity, eventually conceded to freelancer approach

### Impact
- Conference booth (HR Tech Conference, April 14) will have no dedicated page to direct traffic to
- PeopleForce launched a competing page and is already capturing search traffic DataPeople cannot respond to
- Conversion rate on the homepage: 1–1.5% vs HRbox's 3.5%
- Finished design sat unused for over 2 months while the debate continued

### Why it persists
Engineering capacity is fully committed to product work (dashboards, NetSuite connections, bug fixes). Marketing requests are not in the engineering planning cycle, so they arrive as unplanned interruptions. No process exists to formally prioritize cross-team requests. The communication channel mismatch (Marina sent requirements by email; Dave missed them) added further delay.

## Problem 3: Fragmented data and decisions not recorded

### Summary
Critical business information — client records, deal status, financial data, and decisions made in meetings — is scattered across email, spreadsheets, the CRM, and individual notes. This causes lost deals, missed legal obligations, repeated re-discussion of already-made decisions, and a manual, error-prone financial reporting process.

### Occurrences across transcripts

| Date | Meeting | What happened |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Mar 3 | Weekly Sync | LogisPro ownership unclear — Katie started it, handed off to Max on vacation, unknown if Max ever acted. "Everything's in email, in threads" |
| Mar 5 | Sales 1-on-1 | CRM does not track inbound emails. Reps must manually link emails to deals. They don't have time. Half the CRM records are outdated (Anna's own admission) |
| Mar 10 | Weekly Sync | Indeed partnership: Paul and Anna cannot remember if a final decision was made |
| Mar 10 | Weekly Sync | PharmaCorp data protection doc: Owen asked Dave 2 weeks ago, Dave asked Sasha, Sasha never did it. Client writes daily; deal is blocked |
| Mar 13 | Client Call | ProStandard sent a formal complaint to legal@datapeople.com 10 days ago. No one responded. Paul: "Nobody flagged that for me." Contract required a 5-business-day response |
| Mar 17 | Weekly Sync | CargoTransit lost partly because Danny's emails fell through the cracks — emailed Dave (buried), emailed Kevin (deflected as not his area) |
| Mar 19 | Product Standup | Owen's legal data request posted in general chat, got lost. Mike saw it, assumed someone else would handle it. Nobody did |
| Mar 20 | Strategy Session | Steve: financial data lives in 4 places (CRM, Anna's spreadsheets, accounting, Kevin's notes). Last board prep took 2 weeks because numbers didn't match. Kevin counts client losses by accounts; Steve counts by revenue — no agreed method |
| Mar 24 | Weekly Sync | Indeed partnership: Paul again asks if a decision was made — third time this issue came up across meetings |
| Mar 24 | Weekly Sync | NetSuite automation: Paul thinks it was agreed as a priority; Kevin and Dave say it was only added to a list |

### Who raised it
- **Anna Sullivan (VP Sales):** CRM hygiene, client handoffs
- **Steve Morrison (CFO):** financial data fragmentation, manual reporting
- **Owen Nash (General Counsel):** missed legal deadlines, no client contact registry
- **Paul Crawford (CEO):** surfaces the symptom (forgotten decisions, missed client issues) without addressing root cause

### Impact
- Lost client deals due to dropped handoffs and unanswered messages
- ProStandard (150K/year) escalated to CEO level over an unanswered complaint — trust damaged
- Board prep requires 2 weeks of manual data reconciliation every quarter, with mismatched numbers
- Decisions made in meetings are relitigated across multiple subsequent calls, wasting leadership time
- Legal requests fall through the cracks, creating compliance exposure

### Why it persists
No single source of truth exists for deals, decisions, or client records. The CRM is used inconsistently. Meeting decisions are not formally documented or assigned owners. Internal requests routed through email and general chat channels are easy to miss. The team is small and fast-moving, and process discipline is consistently deprioritized in favor of shipping product.

## Recommended Actions for the CEO

### Problem 1 — Slow and missed responses to inbound leads

**Immediate (this week)**
- Schedule a 30-minute meeting with Dave to define a concrete deliverable: an email triage or lead prioritization tool. This has been discussed since March 5 with no output. Set a deadline and an owner.
- As a stopgap, define a response SLA for inbound leads (e.g., 4 hours) and ask Anna to track it manually for 2 weeks. This makes the cost of inaction visible in numbers.

**Short-term (this month)**
- Evaluate AI-based email prioritization tools (e.g., a layer on top of Gmail/Outlook that flags hot leads) — these exist off the shelf and require no custom development. Dave and Anna should assess together within 2 weeks.
- Consider temporarily reassigning Nick's training time to lead response coverage while a solution is built. The team cannot afford to lose Katie.

**Structural**
- Require CRM logging of all inbound leads at the moment of first contact, not retrospectively. This is a precondition for any future automated solution.

---

### Problem 2 — Marketing landing page blocked by dev capacity

**Immediate (this week)**
- Confirm with Dave that Jenny is unambiguously assigned to the landing page starting April 1. Get it in writing (task in the tracker, not a verbal commitment in a meeting).
- Marina should brief Jenny directly before April 1 so no time is lost on onboarding.

**Short-term**
- Run a post-mortem on why a finished design sat unused for 2.5 months. The root cause — marketing requests are invisible to engineering planning — needs a fix, not just a workaround.

**Structural**
- Create a lightweight intake process for cross-team requests: marketing (and legal) submit requests to a shared tracker with business justification and deadline. Engineering reviews it in sprint planning, not ad hoc. This eliminates the pattern of repeated verbal requests getting lost or ignored.
- Set a standing rule: any request with a hard external deadline (conference, client commitment) gets a written owner and delivery date within 48 hours of being raised.

---

### Problem 3 — Fragmented data and decisions not recorded

**Immediate (this week)**
- Appoint one person to write up decisions from each weekly sync as a 5-line summary posted in the team chat within 24 hours. This costs almost nothing and eliminates the pattern of re-litigating the same decisions (Indeed partnership, NetSuite automation priority).
- Ask Owen to audit how legal@datapeople.com is monitored. A client complaint sitting for 10 days with no response is a contract and trust risk that cannot recur.

**Short-term**
- Mandate CRM use for all active deals with a 48-hour logging rule. Anna has been asking for this; it needs to come from Paul, not from Anna.
- For board prep, work with Steve to identify the 5–6 metrics that matter and build a single shared spreadsheet that each owner (Anna, Kevin, Dave, Marina) updates weekly. This replaces the quarterly scramble.

**Structural**
- Agree on unified definitions for key metrics before the board meeting (e.g., client loss = accounts lost, not revenue lost — or the reverse). Kevin and Steve using different methods makes leadership data untrustworthy.
- Consider a lightweight internal wiki or decision log where major choices (partnerships, roadmap priorities, pricing decisions) are recorded with date and rationale. Two to three sentences per decision is enough to stop the cycle of "wait, did we decide this?"
